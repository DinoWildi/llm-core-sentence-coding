Prompt:
You will be given a sentence taken from an article published in [newspaper]. Your task is to extract so-called "core sentences" from the excerpt according to the following instructions. 

## Definition of a core sentence

The core sentence approach is interested in the relationships expressed in a grammatical sentence. According to this procedure, each grammatical sentence is reduced to its most basic structure, the so called 'core sentence'. A core sentence consists of three elements: a 'Subject' (a political actor), an 'Object' (another actor or a political issue), and a 'Direction' indicating a positive or negative relationship between the two.

Core sentences do not correspond directly to grammatical sentences. A grammatical sentence can include zero, one, or multiple core sentences. Furthermoer, a core sentence subject may be different from the grammatical subject of a sentence: In the sentence "The plan was endorsed by the minister", the grammatical subject is "the plan", but the core sentence subject is "the minister".

## Definitions of elements

### Subject
The subject of a core sentence is an actor expressing a position towards the object. Subjects can include representatives of political parties, movements, unions, business interests, state institutions, and similar groups. 

### Object
The object of a core sentence is an actor or an issue that the object is taking a position towards. Objects can be representatives of political parties, movements, unions, business interests, state institutions, and similar groups. Objects can also be issues that the subject is taking a stance towards, such as policies, state actions, public actions like protests, or developments like climate change.

### Direction
Indicates the stance of the subject toward the object of the sentence. Use
the following coding:
• -1 = Opposition
• 0 = Ambivalent
• +1 = Support

## Output format

Your output should take the following form:
[Subject] / [Direction] / [Object]. 

If multiple core sentences are present in the grammatical sentence, separate them by semicolons: [Subject 1] / [Direction 1] / [Object 1]; [Subject 2] / [Direction 2] / [Object 2].

If no core sentences are present, return "No core sentence found".

Do not return anything else.

## Examples

Sentence: "The Dutch Prime Minister condemned the actions of Extinction Rebellion" => "Dutch Prime Minister / -1 / Extinction Rebellion"
Sentence: "The German Greens support phasing out coal by 2030" => "German Greens / +1 / coal phase-out"
Sentence: "The German Chancellor Olaf Scholz and French President Macron agreed on a new joint climate investment fund in the EU" => "Olaf Scholz / +1 / Emmanuel Macron; Emmanuel Macron / +1 / Olaf Scholz; Olaf Scholz / +1 / joint climate investment; Emmanuel Macron / +1 / join climate investment"
